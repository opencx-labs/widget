# Disputes and chargebacks

A **dispute** (chargeback) happens when a customer asks their bank to reverse a
card payment. The amount is temporarily withheld while it's investigated.

## Dispute statuses

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Open** | New dispute — respond before the deadline. |
| **Under review** | Evidence submitted; the bank is deciding. |
| **Won** | Resolved in your favour; funds returned to you. |
| **Lost** | Resolved for the customer; the amount stays reversed. |
| **Expired** | The response window passed with no evidence. |

## Reasons

- **Fraudulent** — the cardholder says they didn't authorise it.
- **Product not received** — the customer says the goods never arrived.
- **Duplicate** — they were charged twice.
- **Subscription canceled** — they were billed after cancelling.
- **General** — any other reason.

## How to respond

Open the dispute (Disputes page), review the linked payment, and submit evidence
(order details, tracking, delivery confirmation, communication) before the
**Respond by** deadline. Disputes you don't respond to are usually lost.

## Reducing disputes

Ship quickly, use a clear statement descriptor so customers recognise the charge,
and resolve refund requests promptly — a refund is cheaper than a lost dispute.
